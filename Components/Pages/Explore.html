Mount Everest

Early Exploration (1800s–1920s)

Everest was identified as the world’s highest mountain in 1856 by the Great Trigonometrical Survey. For decades, Nepal and Tibet restricted access, so early expeditions approached from Tibet. The first serious British expeditions began in the 1920s.

The Mystery of Mallory & Irvine (1924)

In 1924, George Mallory and Andrew Irvine attempted to summit Everest. They disappeared during their ascent, and it remains unknown if they reached the summit before perishing. Mallory’s body was found in 1999, but Irvine’s remains have never been located.

First Successful Ascent (1953)

On May 29, 1953, Sir Edmund Hillary of New Zealand and Tenzing Norgay, a Sherpa of Nepal, became the first climbers confirmed to reach the summit of Mount Everest. Their successful ascent marked a historic milestone in mountaineering and opened the door for future expeditions to the world’s highest peak.
